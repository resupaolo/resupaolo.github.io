PHILIPPINES

Explore the Philippines

PLACES

The Philippines is a well-known tourism destination worldwide. In addition to having stunning landscapes and popular tourist destinations, it has a vibrant cultural heritage. To honor their fellow citizens' heroic deeds and artfully display their past, the Filipinos created a variety of landmarks and locations. These well-known architectural masterpieces have the power to seize spectators' emotions in addition to their eyes. The Philippines' tourist industry includes these historical locations.

RIZAL PARK

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

Rizal Park, also known as Luneta Park, was created as a monument to Dr. Jose Rizal, our greatest national hero, and was once known as Bagumbayan Field. The place where Rizal was put to death by the Spanish troops is one of the most important historical sites in the Philippines.

CORREGIDOR

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

Corregidor, sometimes called "the Rock," is well-known for having significant historical landmarks. When the Japanese invaded the Philippines, Corregidor was chosen as the Allied Forces' main base of operations and the administrative center for the Philippine Commonwealth.

INTRAMUROS

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

Because of its most notable characteristic, a roughly three-mile-long circuit of enormous stone walls and defenses that nearly fully encircles the whole neighborhood, Intramuros is known historically as the "Walled City."

EDSA SHRINE

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

A memorial honoring the first People Power Revolution and its peaceful conclusion on December 15, 1989, is located there. It was the scene of the two protests that led to the ouster of Ferdinand Marcos and Joseph Estrada as presidents.

BARASOAIN CHURCH

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

The Malolos Congress, the first congress in the Philippines, which was convened on September 15, 1898, under the leadership of Pedro Paterno, used this building as its session hall. Augustinian Missionaries founded it in 1859.

LEYTE LANDING

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

The fulfillment of Gen. McArthur's pledge to the Filipinos to return and aid them in defeating the Japanese colonies is one of the park's most popular draws for tourists. It always brings back moving recollections of the heroic battles our cherished ancestors waged to secure our freedom.

FORT SANTIAGO

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

In the Philippines, Fort Santiago in Intramuros is a well-known tourist site. It is a historic building component of the renowned city wall. The site's main draw is a museum with a facsimile of Dr. Jose Rizal's jail cell before his execution.

MACTAN SHRINE

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

The Mactan Shrine commemorated the Battle of Mactan, Lapu-Lapu, Ferdinand Magellan, and Mactan Island in Cebu. It is sometimes referred to as Liberty Shrine and is located at the exact spot where the fight was fought.

RIZAL SHRINE

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

Dr. Jose Rizal spent four years in exile in Dapitan and is commemorated in the Rizal Shrine. He was a doctor, a merchant, a farmer, an inventor, a painter, a sculptor, an archaeologist, a linguist, a grammarian, a teacher, and many other more while he lived here.

BANAUE RICE
TERRACES

Historical places. (n.d.). Home [Facebook page]. Facebook. Retrieved January 9, 2022, from https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100065017471198

The indigenous inhabitants carved the Banaue Rice Terraces into the mountains some 2000 years ago, using just their hands and some essential tools.
