DIFFERENT ETHNIC GROUPS IN THE PHILIPPINES

ATI

The Western and Central Visayas are home to most majoritymajority of the Ati, a Negrito ethnic group. On Panay Island, there are significant populations in Aklan, Capiz, Antique, and Iloilo, with Iloilo housing the most important number.

Ati. Visayas: Remaining Treasures of the Indigenous People. (n.d.). Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://sites.google.com/site/humanities2visayas2015/home/western-visayas/ati

AKLANON

The Aklanons live in the province of Aklan, which is located in the western Visayas region of Panay Island. They converse in Akeanon and Ibayjanon, which are dialects of Hiligaynon.

Aklanon. Visayas: Remaining Treasures of the Indigenous People. (n.d.). Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://sites.google.com/site/humanities2visayas2015/home/western-visayas/aklanons

BADJAO

The Badjao, often referred to as the "Sea Gypsies" of the Sulu and Celebes Seas, are dispersed around the coastlines of Tawi Tawi, Sulu, Basilan, and a few coastal municipalities in Zamboanga del Sur in the ARMM.

RemySmithUK. (2019, July 28). Badjao. Pinterest. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://www.pinterest.ph/pin/844847211329683488/

BICOLANO

There are five dialects of the Bicolano language: Buhi'non, Daraga, Libon, Oas, and Ligao. According to the Central Philippine Languages, it is a member of the Inland Buhi-Daraga subgroup of the Bikol language family. West Albay, Buhi, and a few locations in Camarines Sur are home to Bicolano speakers.

Mostrey, D. (2018, January 3). Bicolano, Albay: Articles. Puerto Parrot. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://www.puertoparrot.com/articles/bicolano-albay

ILOCANO

Ilocanos make up the 3rd largest ethnolinguistic group in the Philippines. Large populations are found in Ilocos Norte, Ilocos Sur, Cagayan, Abra, La Union, Nueva Vizcaya, Pangasinan, Tarlac, and Benguet.

Mostrey, D. (2018, January 3). Ilocano: Articles. Puerto Parrot. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://www.puertoparrot.com/articles/ilocano

MARANAO

The Maranao inhabit Lanao del Norte and Lanao del Sur in Mindanao. The name Maranao translates to mean “People of the Lake”, after their traditional territory in the area surrounding Lake Lanao in the Bukidnon-Lanao Plateau.

Maranao. MARANAO: A SUPERSTITIOUS TRIBE. (n.d.). Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://maranaocultureblog.wordpress.com/
