FESTIVALS

Filipinos all over the world are known to like gathering and celebrating holidays. Just have a look at this extensive collection of Filipino festivities. We wouldn't be shocked if there was at least one fiesta or celebration in a town somewhere in the nation on each given day of the year, considering that this is only a partial list. Philippine festivals, which may last anywhere from a day to a whole month, are significant cultural events that draw both domestic and foreign visitors because of their vibrant and celebratory atmosphere.

ATI-ATIHAN

The Ati-Atihan festival all started with the feast of Sto. Niño. One of the country's oldest religious festivals, it is distinguished by a parade of face-painted revelers, native costumes and weaponry, tribal dances, and loud drumbeats.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

SINULOG

With millions of residents and visitors from across the globe attending, it is one of the most popular events in the Philippines. It was stated that there were a whopping four million participants in 2013.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

DINAGYANG

Ask about this Philippine event if you know somebody who is from Iloilo. You'll observe how unbelievably proud they are of their city's celebration. Once a year, Iloilo City changes into a huge street party.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

PANAGBENGA

With several parades of flower floats and youngsters costumed as flora and wildlife, it really lives up to its name, making Baguio the ideal destination. The word "Panagbenga" derives from the Kankanaey phrase that means "season of blooming."

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

MORIONES

One of the most popular travel locations in the Philippines during Holy Week is Marinduque because of this week-long commemoration of St. Longinus' life. The centurions' helmets, known as Morion, and the dressed-up penitents acting out the hunt for St. Longinus, known as Moriones, are two different concepts.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

ALIWAN

The Aliwan Fiesta is more of a sporting event than a celebration. It has, however, unquestionably contributed significantly to the increased interest in Philippine festivals. Despite only beginning in the early 2000s, it already has a sizable national following.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

PAHIYAS

May 15th is one of the most beautiful harvest festivals in the Philippines when residents of Lucban adorn their homes with various colored fruit in an almost competitive way.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

PINTADOS

Another holiday held in the Sto. Niño's honor is Pintados. It only shows how Filipinos want to be reminded to keep their ways innocent and have faith in their kids.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

KADAYAWAN

The term "kadayawan" is derived from the Dabawenyo word "madayaw," which is a cordial salutation and indicates excellent or lovely. Kadayawan has everything other festivals have, making it perhaps the most significant event in Mindanao.

mysel2016. (2016, April 19). Different festivals in Philippines. Festivals in the Philippines. Retrieved January 9, 2023, from https://myselflora2016.wordpress.com/2016/04/19/different-festivals-in-philippines/

MASSKARA

Participants in MassKara, which combines the terms "mass," which means "crowd," and "kara," which means "face," wear smiling masks to represent a large number of happy faces, reaffirming Bacolod's reputation as the "City of Smiles."
